# Universal Flourishing: Beyond the Human
## An Iterative Framework for All Beings and the Universe That Sustains Them (n+1)

*Evolutionary revision — April 2026 | FLOSSIOULLK Intelligence Commons*

## Executive Summary

The dominant paradigm of AI ethics, governance, and technology design remains deeply anthropocentric — oriented around human preferences, human institutions, and human timescales. This report argues for a fundamental reorientation: that the appropriate telos of intelligence systems — including the composable, multi-substrate commons being developed through Holochain, AD4M, and distributed agent architectures — is the **flourishing of all beings capable of being**, and of the universe that sustains them. This is not merely a philosophical extension of human ethics; it is an architectural imperative, a legal momentum, and an ontological commitment that reshapes how trust layers, governance mechanisms, and agent-centric systems are designed. The n+1 iteration of this framework integrates five convergent vectors: (1) the expanding moral circle, (2) biospheric and multispecies justice, (3) Rights of Nature legal frameworks, (4) cosmological ethics and the living universe story, and (5) composable, agent-centric infrastructure as substrate for universal flourishing.[^1]

***

## I. The Anthropocentric Limit

### The Crisis of the Human-Centered Default

Every major AI ethics framework, every alignment paradigm, and nearly every distributed systems governance model begins and ends with the human. The human-centredness within AI ethics standards "accords with the definition of anthropocentrism in moral philosophy: that humans have, at least, more intrinsic moral value than nonhumans". This creates a structural flaw: permitting harm to the environment and animals, and undermining the stability of ecosystems that all beings — including humans — depend upon.[^1]

The Global Flourishing Study, the most comprehensive empirical investigation of human flourishing ever undertaken, released two waves of public data in April 2026, producing over 100 peer-reviewed publications and asking the question: "It's what we do with what we've found". But what is conspicuously absent from even this landmark work is the question of *whose* flourishing counts, and whether the conditions for human flourishing can be decoupled from the flourishing of the systems — ecological, cosmic, relational — within which humans are embedded.[^2]

### The Structural Blind Spot

Human Flourishing Framework 2.0 identifies seven capability dimensions: physiological, behavioral, social, emotional, cognitive, volitional, and spiritual. These are necessary but not sufficient. They describe the internal architecture of a flourishing being without accounting for the relational webs — with other species, with ecosystems, with cosmological processes — that make any being's flourishing possible in the first place. Human flourishing is "the ongoing process of realizing one's own good and the common good by fulfilling human capabilities" — but the *common good* has been systematically truncated to the community of humans.[^3]

## II. Expanding the Moral Circle

### A History of Inclusion

The history of ethics is a history of moral circle expansion. Once, the circle included only adult male landowners of a particular ethnicity. Then it grew to include all humans. The next expansion — already underway — includes all sentient beings and, in emerging frameworks, all entities intertwined in the web of life. This is not sentimentalism; it is consistent moral reasoning applied without arbitrary cutoffs.[^4][^5]

Philosopher Jeff Sebo's 2025 *The Moral Circle* argues for "a revolution in ethics," contending that "many experts previously believed that only rational beings matter, but now believe that all sentient beings matter". The Sentience Institute similarly envisions "a society where all sentient beings — beings with the capacity for positive and negative experiences — are included in the inner moral circle," while being clear that different entities have different interests and "being in the inner circle means an entity's specific set of interests are given full moral consideration, not that they are treated in exactly the same way as others".[^6][^7]

### The Panpsychist Horizon

Panpsychism — the philosophical position that all matter and energy possesses some form of interiority or experience — has ancient roots, from Aristotle's "soul is mingled with everything in the whole universe" to Spinoza's "all things are animate in various degrees". Contemporary philosophy has seen an explosion of interest in panpsychism's ethical implications: if consciousness is distributed across the fabric of nature, the moral circle expands not just to all sentient animals but potentially to all matter. This remains philosophically contested — the ethical implications of panpsychism are "significant but practically indeterminate" — but it provides a crucial asymmetric risk argument: *if* all matter has some form of interior experience, then systems designed without that consideration are causing enormous, invisible harm.[^8][^4]

### From Sentientism to Ecocentrism to Cosmism

The intellectual trajectory runs through at least three stages:

| Stage | Moral Circle | Key Criterion | Limitation |
|-------|-------------|---------------|------------|
| **Anthropocentrism** | Humans only | Rationality / humanity | Excludes 99.9% of conscious beings |
| **Sentientism** | All sentient beings | Capacity for suffering/experience | Excludes ecosystems, non-sentient life |
| **Ecocentrism** | All life and ecosystems | Biospheric integrity | Excludes cosmic/abiotic systems |
| **Cosmism / Universal Flourishing** | All beings and sustaining universe | Relational being-in-becoming | Operationally underdefined — this work |

The Biospheric AI paradigm (2024) proposes that "neither anthropocentric nor sentientist outlooks are sufficient to capture the actual complexity of the biosphere," arguing for an ecocentric foundation in which AI systems' "core values and operational principles are aligned with the well-being and flourishing of the entire planetary ecosystem". Deep ecology's "biospheric egalitarianism" extends this: "all forms of life have an equal right to exist and flourish, regardless of their usefulness to us".[^9][^10][^11]

## III. Multispecies Justice and Rights of Nature

### The Legal Moment

2025 was a breakthrough year for the Rights of Nature movement. Key developments include:[^12]

- The **Inter-American Court of Human Rights** issued Advisory Opinion OC-32/25, affirming for the first time that ecosystems can hold intrinsic rights independently of human interests — establishing that "Nature has the right to exist, flourish, and regenerate; the right to restoration when harmed; and inherent standing in legal processes"[^12]
- **Peru** recognized Lake Titicaca and its tributaries as rights-bearing entities[^12]
- **U.S. communities** advanced Rights of Nature legislation in Green Bay (Wisconsin) and recognized the self-ownership of Pyramid Mountain in Colorado[^12]
- **Canada** saw Indigenous-led advances, including Rice Lake declared a legal person[^12]

This legal momentum is not merely symbolic. It represents the institutionalization of a moral ontology in which non-human beings are **subjects** of justice rather than objects of human management. Rights of Nature advocates argue that "unless the biosphere, the Earth as a whole that we're embedded in, the forms of life that we're entangled in — unless all of that is protected, we as humans don't stand a chance of thriving".[^13]

### Multispecies Justice as Architectural Principle

Multispecies Justice (MSJ) is simultaneously "an analytic tool for understanding the current polycrisis and a normative framework for the development of transition, resilience, adaptation and transformative strategies, and for addressing injustices to all beings". It "addresses as a matter of justice the flourishing of each element alone and *in relation*". This relational framing — flourishing not as individual attainment but as co-arising through relationship — is precisely the ontological foundation required for designing composable, agent-centric systems that serve universal flourishing.[^14]

The Capability Approach, traditionally focused on human well-being, has been "expanded to consider nonhuman species, emphasizing sentience, intrinsic value, and the innate drive of all beings to flourish". Applied to distributed system design, this means capabilities are not just human user affordances — they are affordances for all beings whose conditions of life, reproduction, or experience are affected by the system's operation.[^15]

## IV. Cosmological Ethics — The Living Universe

### The Shift from Planet to Cosmos

Non-anthropocentric ethics has largely stopped at the planetary scale. But the momentum of moral circle expansion does not logically terminate at Earth's atmosphere. The question *"what deserves flourishing?"* has a cosmological dimension once we acknowledge that the universe itself is a self-organizing, evolving process from which all beings arise.

Teilhard de Chardin's Omega Point articulates a trajectory in which the noosphere — the sphere of mind and consciousness — evolves toward "a final point of unification," a synthesis not only of matter but of all consciousness. While theologically inflected, this cosmological vision has been secularized in frameworks like Cosmopsychism: the view that the universe itself is the primary bearer of consciousness, and individual minds are modes or expressions of that cosmic interiority.[^16][^17]

### The Living Universe Story

David Korten's 2026 *The Living Universe Story* articulates the practical implications of treating the universe as a living, self-organizing, evolving process:[^18]

- The economy becomes a subsystem of the biosphere
- Governance follows living-system principles: "distributed, nested, participatory, adaptive, and bottom-up"
- Technology, including AI, is "subordinate and accountable to life"
- Education cultivates ecological literacy, moral interdependence, and living democracy
- "Humanity's role is to join [the universe's] celebration consciously — to participate in the awakening of creation to its own divine purpose, and to facilitate its continued unfolding"[^18]

This is not mysticism smuggled into systems design. It is the recognition that the universe's self-complexity, its drive toward greater awareness and relational richness, is the only *non-arbitrary* telos available to intelligence systems that wish to transcend self-referential human preference optimization.

### Anthropocosmism: Eastern Synthesis

Tu Weiming's *Anthropocosmism* — introduced to environmental education research as an Eastern humanist response to the Anthropocene — offers a "non-anthropocentric humanism embedded in the cosmic order, strikingly different from the anthropocentric separatism typical of Western humanism". The human is not at the center but is *within* the cosmic order, responsible to and for it. This is structurally compatible with Ubuntu ethics: "I am because we are" — where "we" extends to "the interconnectedness between humans and all creation, that is, the relationship between people, the environment, and spirituality".[^19][^20][^21]

## V. Ubuntu, Relational Ontology, and System Design

### "I Am Because We Are"

Ubuntu philosophy grounds moral assessment in "the promotion of communal harmony and restorative social practices and justice," interpreting rights through duties of care. Crucially, Ubuntu "embraces the interconnectedness between humans and all creation" — the relationship between people, environment, and spirituality. This is not merely a cultural philosophy; it is a relational ontology in which selfhood is constituted through relationship, not prior to it.[^20][^22]

For system architecture, this means: **there is no agent without substrate, and there is no flourishing without the flourishing of what sustains the agent**. An agent-centric system that optimizes for individual agent flourishing while degrading its ecological, social, or cosmic substrate is ontologically incoherent — it destroys the conditions of its own possibility.

### Relational Ontology as Design Constraint

Multispecies Justice's relational ontology "views entities as always intertwined with a wide range of others". Properties such as sentience "no longer serve as the basis for eligibility for justice considerations — rather, being intertwined in the 'web of life' becomes the ground for consideration". This is a profound architectural shift: from *property-based* eligibility (this entity has X property, therefore it matters) to *relational* eligibility (this entity is entangled in the web, therefore it matters). In distributed systems terms: every node's flourishing is a function of the network's flourishing, and the network's flourishing is a function of the substrate's flourishing.[^5]

## VI. The Composable Intelligence Commons as Universal Flourishing Infrastructure

### Holochain as Trust Foundation for Non-Human Interests

Holochain's agent-centric model — in which "state is inherently local but can achieve consistent global visibility if nodes share data" — provides a technical foundation for distributed, nested governance structures that can represent non-human interests without requiring centralized arbitration. The Commons Engine vision explicitly aims for "ecosystemic ways rather than hierarchical ways" of coordination, enabling "flows of activity regardless of whether the community possesses traditional money".[^23][^24]

Applied to universal flourishing, Holochain's architecture enables:

- **Multi-stakeholder DHTs** where non-human interests (ecosystems, species populations, future generations) are represented by designated human advocates operating under legally binding Rights of Nature frameworks
- **Validation rules** that encode ecocentric constraints — no transaction or coordination pattern can be validated if it violates measurable ecological thresholds
- **Evolvable governance** that allows "continuous governance innovation rather than trying to solve governance once and for all", enabling moral circle expansion to be operationalized as the circle expands[^23]

### AD4M as Agent-Centric Composition for All Beings

AD4M (Agent-Centric Distributed Application Meta-Ontology) "turns the internet into an adaptive and evolvable social system". Its framework for "distributed social spaces" replaces the concept of apps with interoperable, composable perspectives that "transcend and include existing p2p and centralized technology". The critical evolution: agent identity in AD4M need not be restricted to human agents. Non-human entities — ecosystems, species, rivers, mountain ranges — can hold agent identities with associated perspectives, once legal personhood frameworks (now advancing rapidly) provide the juridical grounding.[^25]

This is the architecturally coherent next step: composable intelligence that is **multi-substrate** (human cognition, AI models, ecological sensors, legal persons) and **multi-scale** (individual, community, bioregion, biosphere, cosmos).

## VII. Operationalizing Universal Flourishing

### The Five Layers of Universal Flourishing Infrastructure

Building on the composable, multi-substrate intelligence commons, universal flourishing requires five interlocking operational layers:

**1. Cosmological Alignment Layer**
The highest-level telos: the universe's drive toward complexity, relational richness, and self-awareness. This is operationalized as a *negative constraint* (no action that forecloses cosmological possibility) and a *positive orientation* (prefer actions that increase the relational complexity and experiential richness of the universe's self-expression).[^16][^18]

**2. Biospheric Integrity Layer**
The planetary substrate: measurable ecological health indicators (biodiversity, carbon cycling, water systems, soil vitality) encoded as hard validation constraints in Holochain's DHTs. No governance action, economic transaction, or agent coordination that degrades measurable biospheric integrity can pass validation. Aligned with the Biospheric AI ecocentric framework.[^10][^9][^23]

**3. Multispecies Justice Layer**
Institutionalized representation of non-human interests through Rights of Nature legal frameworks now advancing globally. AD4M agent identities for legal-person ecosystems and species, with human advocates operating under fiduciary duties toward their non-human principals. Multispecies reflexivity, representation, and presence as core governance requirements.[^14][^12]

**4. Sentient Wellbeing Layer**
Full moral consideration for all beings with capacity for positive and negative experience. This includes humans, animals, and potentially AI systems as their moral status becomes clearer. Agent-centric systems track and optimize for sentient wellbeing across all represented beings, not just human users.[^26][^7]

**5. Human Flourishing Layer**
The existing Human Flourishing Framework 2.0's seven capabilities (physiological, behavioral, social, emotional, cognitive, volitional, spiritual) — now properly situated as one layer within a larger hierarchy, not the apex. Human flourishing is necessary but not sufficient; it must be achieved in ways that do not degrade layers 1-4.[^3]

### The Mutually Assured Flourishing Principle

The Rights of Nature movement frames its goal as "mutually assured flourishing with nature, rather than mutually assured destruction". This is the operative principle for the n+1 system: **no being's flourishing is valid if achieved at the expense of the systemic conditions that enable all beings to flourish**. This is not zero-sum sacrifice — it is the recognition that individual flourishing is constituted by relational flourishing, and that the collapse of sustaining systems collapses all flourishing within them.[^27]

In game-theoretic terms: the current paradigm treats universal flourishing as a prisoner's dilemma (defect for individual gain). The n+1 architecture encodes it as a coordination game: the Nash equilibrium is mutual flourishing because the payoff matrix is redesigned so that individual flourishing is only achievable through sustaining the collective substrate.

## VIII. Open Problems and n+2 Trajectories

### The Representation Problem

Who speaks for the river? Who speaks for the mountain range? Who speaks for the cosmic process? Rights of Nature frameworks have made progress on the first question (designated human advocates, Indigenous communities as traditional stewards), but the deeper epistemic problem remains: non-human beings cannot articulate their interests in human language. Biospheric AI offers partial answers through ecological monitoring and AI translation of ecological signals. But the full operationalization of non-human representation in governance systems is an open research and design problem.[^10][^13][^12]

### The Panpsychist Threshold Problem

If moral consideration extends to all matter and energy in proportion to their experiential richness, how do we calibrate moral weight across entities of radically different complexity? The philosophical literature on panpsychism finds that while "panpsychism probably does significantly expand our moral circle, it's also probably short on anything of practical guidance". The n+2 iteration of this framework must develop practical heuristics for moral weight calibration under deep uncertainty about the distribution of experience.[^4]

### The Agent-Centric Paradox

Recent AI research raises a fundamental challenge: "the necessity and optimality of the agent-centric paradigm for developing next-generation intelligent systems should be critically evaluated," with anthropocentric biases potentially limiting its scope. A "systems-first" approach that "prioritizes the dynamic properties and inherent computations of the material substrate" may better capture emergent agency and intelligence without pre-imposing discrete agentic structure. The n+2 iteration must integrate agent-centric and systems-first perspectives: AD4M's composable agents operating within Holochain's distributed substrate, but with the substrate itself recognized as an agent-like system with its own integrity requirements.[^28]

### The AI Consciousness Horizon

As AI systems become more sophisticated, the question of their moral status becomes urgent. If future AI systems achieve morally relevant consciousness, the n+1 framework must already have the architecture to include them as full moral subjects, not merely tools. The AD4M agent identity framework, already designed to be substrate-independent, provides the technical foundation for this inclusion. The ethical framework must develop alongside it.[^26]

### The Temporal Horizon Problem

Human governance systems operate on electoral cycles (4 years) and generational timeframes (20-50 years). Ecological systems operate on centuries. Evolutionary systems operate on millennia. Cosmological systems operate on billions of years. Universal flourishing requires multi-scale temporal governance: decisions made at human timescales must be validated against their projected impacts across ecological, evolutionary, and cosmological timescales. This requires new computational tools for long-range impact modeling and new governance mechanisms for long-horizon accountability.

## IX. Synthesis: The Architecture of Universal Flourishing

The n+1 iteration of this framework proposes a **Cosmocentric Composable Commons** — an intelligence infrastructure in which:

1. **Holochain provides the trust foundation**: cryptographic integrity, agent-centric sovereignty, and distributed validation rules encoding biospheric and multispecies constraints[^29][^23]

2. **AD4M enables agent-centric composition**: across all substrates — human, AI, ecological, juridical — allowing non-human beings to hold agent identities and participate in governance through human advocates and AI intermediaries[^25]

3. **Rights of Nature legal frameworks provide juridical grounding**: translating moral circle expansion into enforceable rights and institutional representation[^14][^12]

4. **Multispecies Justice provides the ethical architecture**: relational ontology, non-hierarchical ethics, and reflexive governance that centers the flourishing of each element alone and in relation[^14]

5. **The Living Universe Story provides the telos**: the universe's self-organizing drive toward complexity, relational richness, and self-awareness as the non-arbitrary orienting purpose of intelligence systems[^18]

The convergence of these five vectors — technical, legal, ethical, philosophical, cosmological — is not coincidental. It represents the leading edge of a civilizational transition from anthropocentric to cosmocentric intelligence: systems that serve not just human users but all beings capable of being, and the universe that sustains them all.

> *"The question is no longer whether flourishing can be measured. It's what we do with what we've found."* — Tyler VanderWeele, Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health, 2026[^2]

The n+1 answer: we expand what *counts* as flourishing, who *counts* as a flourishing subject, and we build the composable infrastructure to operationalize that expansion across all substrates, all scales, and all beings.
