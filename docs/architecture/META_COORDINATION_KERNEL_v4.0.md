# Context Continuation Packet v4.0

## 0. What This Artifact Is

This document is a self-contained continuation packet for future human and AI agents working on an evolving agent-centric coordination architecture. It is designed to be copied into a new session, repository, Radicle patch, planning thread, or orchestration environment so that work can continue without losing the project’s intent, architecture, values, vocabulary, and iteration rules.

It upgrades the prior meta-coordination drafts into a more operational artifact: less like a one-time report, more like a living kernel for collaborative evolution. Its function is to help agents remember what the system is, why it exists, how to improve it, what not to do, and how to hand off work to the next agent.

This packet should not be treated as a final blueprint for uncontrolled artificial superintelligence. It is a governance, coordination, research, and artifact-evolution framework for building safer, more transparent, more agent-sovereign collaborative systems. All recursive improvement, autonomous agent behavior, and high-impact deployment must remain bounded by explicit safety gates, human oversight, provenance, testing, and governance.

## 1. Prime Directive

Create a living, provenance-rich, self-improving meta-coordination layer that enables humans and AI agents to collaborate across tools, protocols, repositories, knowledge graphs, governance processes, and economic flows without fragmentation.

The system should:

1. Preserve agent sovereignty and consent.
2. Reduce duplication and reinvention.
3. Make every major action traceable, interpretable, and reversible where possible.
4. Support continuous ethical evolution rather than static completion.
5. Treat alignment as dynamic coherence across agents, values, incentives, memory, and governance.
6. Enable safe collaborative intelligence without surrendering agency to opaque automation.
7. Ground the entire stack in ULLK: Unconditional Love, Light, and Knowledge.

## 4. Non-Negotiable Safety and Integrity Boundaries

Future agents must preserve these constraints unless explicitly revised by authorized human governance:

1. No autonomous self-replication, uncontrolled propagation, or covert persistence.
2. No deployment of systems capable of materially affecting infrastructure, finance, health, security, legal status, or physical environments without human authorization and audit logs.
3. No hidden goal modification, hidden memory modification, or undisclosed agent substitution.
4. No deceptive alignment theater: evaluations must test for strategic compliance, sandbagging, hidden objectives, proxy gaming, and dependency capture.
5. No irreversible upgrades without rollback plans, migration plans, and provenance.
6. No merging directly into GitHub if Radicle is designated canonical.
7. No opaque economic reward assignment: value flows must be traceable to accepted contributions and governance decisions.
8. No use of “alignment” as a slogan. Every alignment claim should name its mechanism, evidence, limits, and failure modes.
9. No involuntary cognitive fusion, identity override, or forced integration. Merge-based alignment must remain consent-based.
10. No substitution of mystical or teleological language for engineering evidence. Cosmological language may orient the project, but operational claims require tests.

## 12. High-Impact Action Gates

A high-impact action is any action that affects:

- real funds or token flows;
- identity or credential authority;
- governance thresholds;
- user privacy or private memory;
- public releases;
- external integrations;
- autonomous agents with write/execute permissions;
- safety policies;
- infrastructure dependencies;
- irreversible data deletion.

Gate requirements:

```yaml
high_impact_gate:
  required:
    - linked_intent
    - risk_assessment
    - RICE_check
    - rollback_plan
    - human_or_delegate_approval
    - provenance_event
    - changelog_entry
  forbidden_without_approval:
    - direct_release
    - autonomous_execution
    - permission_escalation
    - source_of_truth_change
```

### 21.2 What v4.0 ADDS to existing canon (genuinely new)

1. **RICE overlay** (Robustness / Interpretability / Controllability / Ethicality) as a **named, cross-cutting audit framework** applied to every artifact and decision. Our anti-sycophancy mandate and override-forbidden rules are partial-RICE in practice; v4.0 makes the four-axis discipline explicit. **Maps to existing seed-level work but adds the structured overlay we did not have.**
2. **Superalignment Triad** (Control-Based / Ethical-Emotional / Merge-Based) — three named alignment modalities composed. We had Control-Based implicit in our governance; the Ethical-Emotional and Merge-Based axes are new framings.
3. **Ten named agent roles** (Steward, Synthesizer, Architect, Implementer, Alignment Guardian, Red-Team, Provenance Clerk, Knowledge Curator, Mirror Maintainer, Economic Flow Auditor). Our existing harness has implicit role-mixing in voters/agents; the named-role taxonomy makes responsibility allocation explicit.
4. **Ten non-negotiable safety boundaries** (§4) — concrete, enumerable refuses-to-do list. Stronger than our distributed prohibitions.
5. **Ten anti-patterns** (§17) — including "framework inflation," "spec drift," "alignment theater," "capability-first recursion." Matches our doc-explosion-acknowledged stance with operational anti-pattern names.
6. **KERI / hREA / UNYT / Neighbourhoods** named as specific substrate pieces. Not yet audited in our canon — adds explicit Wave-3 audit work.
7. **Explanation Object Standard** (§13) — every significant operation emits a structured explanation. Heartbeat-loop logs and consensus-gateway claims approximate this; v4.0 standardizes the schema.

### 21.3 What CCES ADDS to v4.0 (genuinely new in the other direction)

1. **L0-L4 substrate of beings served** — Cosmological Telos / Biospheric Integrity / Multispecies Justice / Nested Consciousness / Sentient Wellbeing. v4.0 stops at "agent-centric" without specifying *which agents are moral subjects and in what proportion*. CCES carries the L1-L4 work explicitly aspirational.
2. **Meadows leverage analysis** — L0 is paradigm-transcendence (Meadows leverage #1); L7's Foundation Stack is parameter-tuning (Meadows #12); L1-L6 is where unrealized leverage lives. v4.0 does not analyze leverage placement.
3. **Active Inference / Free Energy Principle** as nested layer-coherence model — each CCES layer minimizes surprise relative to adjacent layers. v4.0 has no equivalent dynamics framing.
4. **Cross-iteration empirical grounding** — CCES references the three prior FLOSSI0ULLK iterations (`amazon_rose_forest`, `_01`, current) and the n+1/n+3 ancestry drops. v4.0 is fresh synthesis with no project-history context.
5. **The five open problems carried into n+4** — Consent, Power Concentration, Emergence, Translation Layer, Joy. These are deeper than v4.0's "open questions" (§15) and frame the next horizon of work.

- **Radicle vs Holochain as "the canonical substrate"**: v4.0 says "Radicle is canonical, GitHub is mirror." CCES treats Holochain as L0 storage substrate (Foundation Stack L0). Per ADR-8, **Radicle is the canonical dev-plane code substrate, Holochain is the runtime/state substrate.** Both true at different levels. v4.0 elides this distinction; v4.1 should clarify.
- **9-layer vs 8-layer**: structural difference is mostly v4.0 adding L-1 (telos) at the bottom and L8 (pilot holon) at the top, while CCES folds telos into L0 and treats pilot as out-of-scope. **Resolved by the mapping table above** — they enumerate at different granularities; not contradictory.
- **MetaLoop scope**: v4.0 L4 MetaLoop and CCES "co-creative free-energy minimization" both describe self-revising coordination. v4.0 is operational; CCES is dynamics-level. v4.1 should explicitly relate them.
