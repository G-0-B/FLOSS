Given our current conditions, more perfectly neutral procedural fairness will UNDERMINE, not further, political equality. 

 The democratic power approach introduces concepts,such as democratic institutions as mechanisms for organizing collective power, the people, and the state, that help synthesize the modern conditions under which institutional procedures operate. But the democratic power approach also contains a substantive lesson: democratic institutions will, at times, have to treat unfairly the projects of those who want to undermine the collective organization of power. As a result, democrats will often have to choose between procedural fairness and substantive political equality—and they should choose the latter.
